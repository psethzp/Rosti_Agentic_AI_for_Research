The rehabilitation system is designed to train stroke patients in reach and grasp movements through AR-aligned pouring and tracing tasks, incorporating visual, auditory, and haptic feedback (c0001). Significant technical hurdles exist, including anchor drift and inconsistent haptic support, which could compromise the system's reliability (c0001). Solutions are being implemented, such as relocalizing anchors and employing filtering for tilt data (c0003). Furthermore, the system incorporates an adaptive controller designed to adjust task parameters and feedback based on user performance, moving from knowledge of performance to knowledge of results (c0002, c0004). This adaptive approach aims to enhance the rehabilitation experience by dynamically tailoring the challenge and feedback.

Suggested Actions & Next Hypotheses

Directly Measure and Correlate Muscle Activation with Task Performance · NextStep

The core claim is to improve selective muscle activation, yet the evidence focuses on task completion metrics and feedback. A critical next step is to integrate direct muscle activation measurement (e.g., using EMG sensors) and establish a clear correlation between these measurements and the system's performance metrics (e.g., RMS error, SPARC). This will validate the system's primary therapeutic goal.

Investigate the Impact of Anchor Drift on Rehabilitation Efficacy · Hypothesis

Anchor drift is identified as a significant technical challenge. A hypothesis to explore is whether the degree of anchor drift directly impacts the effectiveness of the rehabilitation tasks and the patient's ability to learn and improve motor control. This would involve quantifying drift during sessions and correlating it with patient progress and feedback consistency.

Clarify the Specifics of Adaptive Difficulty and Fading Feedback Based on Muscle Activation · Clarification

The system claims adaptive difficulty and fading feedback are based on muscle activation. However, the evidence is vague. A clarification is needed on *how* muscle activation data will be interpreted and translated into specific adjustments for parameters like corridor width, tilt window, and feedback fading. What are the thresholds and algorithms involved?.

Validate the Reliability of Projected Dashboard Metrics in Real-World Scenarios · NextStep

The projected dashboard is described with specific metrics. A next step is to conduct user testing to validate that these metrics are reliably captured and accurately reflect the patient's performance and progress, especially in the presence of potential technical challenges like anchor drift and noisy tilt estimation. This includes confirming that all mentioned metrics (on path, tilt window, spills proxy, RMS error, SPARC) are indeed captured and displayed.

Explore the Long-Term Efficacy of Gamified AR Rehabilitation for Stroke Patients · Hypothesis

While the system is designed to be intuitive and motivational through gamification, a forward-looking hypothesis is to investigate the long-term adherence and sustained therapeutic benefits of this AR-based approach compared to traditional stroke rehabilitation methods. This would involve longitudinal studies to assess patient engagement and functional recovery over extended periods.
