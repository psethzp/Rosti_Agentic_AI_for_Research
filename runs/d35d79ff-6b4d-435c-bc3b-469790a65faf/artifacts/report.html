i0003 · Confidence 0.95

Summary: Technical challenges in VR implementation, such as anchor stability and haptic consistency, are being addressed through specific engineering solutions and alternative approaches.

Developing an immersive VR rehabilitation system involves overcoming significant technical obstacles. Challenges like anchor drift and noisy tilt estimation are being tackled through methods such as relocalizing anchors at the start of sessions and applying low-pass filters to tilt data (c0003). Inconsistent haptic support is also a concern, with alternative solutions like running tracing without haptics being considered (c0003). The adaptive controller itself requires robust design to manage difficulty oscillations and metric drift, employing techniques like moving averages and buffered writes (c0003). These ongoing efforts highlight the iterative nature of developing reliable VR rehabilitation tools.
